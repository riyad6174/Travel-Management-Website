Western Desert

The Western Desert provided a natural barrier between Egyptian settlements and outside invaders. The immense Western Desert spans the entire western area of ​​Egypt from the Mediterranean Sea in the north to the border with Sudan in the south.

AI Bagawat

The Necropolis of El-Bagawat lies behind the Temple of Hibis in El Kharga Oasis. Built of mud brick, the cemetery comprises more than 260 tombs that resemble small churches from the Christian era. The oldest tomb dates back to the 3rd century and the latest to the 7th century.

Pyramid of Giza

The Giza Pyramids, built to endure an eternity, have done just that. The monumental tombs are relics of Egypt's Old Kingdom era and were constructed some 4,500 years ago. Egypt's pharaohs expected to become gods in the afterlife.
